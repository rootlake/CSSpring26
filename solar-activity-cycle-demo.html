The solar activity cycle

The Sun’s magnetic field waxes and wanes on a cycle of roughly 11 years (the Schwabe cycle). One common measure is the International Sunspot Number: monthly averages rise toward “solar maximum,” then fall toward a quiet “solar minimum,” repeating for decades. The plot below uses monthly means from 1945 onward so several full cycles are visible.
